Didi Zhou

Computer Science at The University of Texas at Austin.

Hi! I’m Didi, a third year studying computer science at The University of Texas at Austin under the Turing Scholars honors program. I’m interested in Natural Language Processing, AI Ethics, and education.

I am the co-lead of Freetail Hackers, an organization that plans HackTX and other events. Previously, I was the Outreach director and was responsible for ensuring our hackathons are beginner friendly and diverse. To that end, we created The Ultimate Hackathon Starting Guide to help people prepare and begin their projects. I’m also a Pod mentor for freshmen CS majors where I help guide freshmen through their first year.

Last summer, I interned at Google on the Flights team working on a data pipeline to suggest dates to travel during special events and holidays. The spring before that, I was at Facebook working on the Messenger Privacy Team adding in a new privacy settings page and adding screenshot detection to vanish mode on the Android app. The summer before that, I was an Engineering Practicum (now STEP) intern at Google, working on adding an action justification setting to the G-Suite Security Center under Google Cloud.
